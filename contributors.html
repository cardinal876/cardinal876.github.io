Join team 10,000! Raysbook creates an encyclopedia with the top 10,000 education-related topics.

Author requirements

A. Doctorate degree in a related field such as a PhD, MD, or JD.

B. Article Length: 150 to 2000 words.

C. Article Level: Between 9th grade high school level and college freshman 101 non-major level.

Compensation for Authors:

1. Raysbook sources brand sponsors who post their names, logos, and slogans (not ads) with the encyclopedia articles.

2. Raysbook pays authors 51% of the revenue from sponsors, but it doesn't pay authors directly. Instead it pools all the authors' revenue into a venture capital and angel investment fund.

3. Each individual author's share of the sponsorship revenue is deteremined by the following formula.

- 2/3 of authors' sponsorship revenue is divided equally among authors by length of article. 2/3 of total revenue * page length of article/ total length of all articles.

- 1/3 of authors' sponsorship revenue is divided by page views. 1/3 of total revenue * author's page views /total page views.

Rays Venture Capital and Angel Investing Fund

4. Raysbook hires a venture capitalist or angel investor, with a history of successful investing, to invest the fund into a single startup with high growth and high potential.

5. For the venture capital and angel investment fund, the authors receive a percentage of the total outcome of the investment. Each author's percentage is determined by the value of his or her total revenue, described in #3, divided by the total initial size of the fund.

6. For the final payout of the investment, the authors receive:

100% of the initial value of the investment fund.

For any growth beyond the initial investment fund size:

- The venture capitalist or angel investor receives 20% (standard)

- Raysbook receives 29%.

- Author receives 51%.

For example, if an author's initial revenue share was $5000, the fund was invested in a startup, and the startup was aquired in three years and had grown to 20x its initial value at the time of investment:

- Author receives $5000 (100% of initial investment) + $48450 (51% of the growth which is .51 x 95,000)= $53,450.
